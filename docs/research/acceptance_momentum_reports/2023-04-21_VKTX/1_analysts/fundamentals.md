# Fundamental Analysis Report: Viking Therapeutics (VKTX)
**Analysis Date:** April 21, 2023
**Company Overview:** Viking Therapeutics, Inc. is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on the development of novel, orally available, and injectable therapies for metabolic and endocrine disorders. Its lead programs include treatments for Non-Alcoholic Steatohepatitis (NASH) and obesity.

---

### 1. Financial Performance and History
Viking Therapeutics is currently in the clinical stage, meaning it generates **zero revenue** from product sales. Its financial health is primarily measured by its research and development (R&D) efficiency and its cash runway.

*   **Net Loss:** For the fiscal year ending December 31, 2022, VKTX reported a net loss of **$68.9 million**, compared to **$55.0 million** in 2021. This widening loss is driven by increased clinical trial activity.
*   **Quarterly Update (Q1 2023):** As of the quarter ending March 31, 2023, the company reported a quarterly net loss of **$19.5 million**. 
*   **Operating Expenses:** R&D expenses rose to **$54.2 million** in 2022 (up from $45.0M in 2021), reflecting the advancement of its key pipeline candidates, VK2809 and VK2735.

### 2. Balance Sheet and Liquidity Analysis
As a pre-revenue biotech, Viking's balance sheet is its lifeline.
*   **Cash Position:** As of March 31, 2023, Viking held approximately **$135.6 million** in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments.
*   **Debt:** The company maintains a very clean balance sheet with no long-term debt, only minor capital lease obligations ($1.49 million).
*   **Cash Runway:** Based on a quarterly burn rate of roughly **$20M–$25M**, Viking has a cash runway extending into the **mid-2024**. This provides enough cushion to reach several clinical milestones without immediate desperate need for dilutive financing, though biotech firms often raise capital following positive data readouts.

### 3. Pipeline and Actionable Insights
The investment thesis for VKTX is currently centered on two major catalysts:

*   **VK2735 (Obesity/T2D):** A dual GLP-1/GIP receptor agonist. In late March 2023 (just weeks ago), VKTX released positive Phase 1 clinical data showing significant weight loss and a favorable safety profile. This positions VKTX as a potential competitor to Eli Lilly’s Mounjaro and Novo Nordisk’s Wegovy.
*   **VK2809 (NASH):** A thyroid hormone receptor beta-selective agonist. It is currently in a Phase 2b trial (VOYAGE). Positive results here could address a multi-billion dollar market with no current FDA-approved therapies.
*   **Market Sentiment:** Analyst sentiment is overwhelmingly bullish. With **12 "Buy"** and **5 "Strong Buy"** ratings, and a mean price target of **$92.72** (against a current price in the $33 range), the market expects significant upside driven by Phase 2 data readouts.

### 4. Risk Factors
*   **Clinical Failure:** As with all biotechs, the primary risk is the potential for clinical trials to fail to meet primary endpoints.
*   **Execution Risk:** Even with positive data, the company faces stiff competition from "Big Pharma" (Eli Lilly, Novo Nordisk) who have significantly more resources for commercialization.
*   **Dilution:** Future equity offerings are likely to fund late-stage Phase 3 trials.
