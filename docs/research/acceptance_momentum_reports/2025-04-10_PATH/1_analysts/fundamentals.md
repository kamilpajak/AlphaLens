### **UiPath Inc. (PATH) Fundamental Analysis Report**
**Date:** April 10, 2025  
**Ticker:** PATH  
**Exchange:** NYSE  

---

#### **1. Company Profile**
UiPath Inc. is a leader in the Robotic Process Automation (RPA) sector, providing an end-to-end automation platform. Its software allows organizations to build, manage, and run "robots" that emulate human actions interacting with digital systems. The company serves a global market, with significant operations in the United States, Romania, and Japan.

#### **2. Financial Performance & Profitability**
UiPath has reached a critical inflection point, transitioning from a high-burn growth phase to a profitable, cash-generating enterprise.

*   **Revenue Growth:** Trailing Twelve Month (TTM) revenue stands at **$1.61 billion**. Recent quarterly results show a strong upward trajectory, with the latest quarter (ending January 2026) reaching **$481.1 million**, a 13.6% year-over-year growth.
*   **High Gross Margins:** The software-infrastructure nature of the business allows for elite gross margins, consistently remaining **above 80%** ($408.3 million gross profit on $481.1 million revenue in the latest quarter).
*   **GAAP Profitability:** After years of losses, UiPath has delivered positive Net Income in recent quarters. The latest fiscal year (2026) reported a Net Income of **$282.3 million**, compared to a loss of $73.7 million the year prior.
*   **Operating Efficiency:** Operating income turned positive at **$80.3 million** in the latest quarter, indicating that the company is successfully scaling its operations while controlling expenses.

#### **3. Cash Flow and Capital Allocation**
The company's cash flow profile is robust, though it is heavily influenced by Stock-Based Compensation (SBC).

*   **Operating Cash Flow:** Consistently positive, generating **$371.2 million** in the most recent fiscal year.
*   **Stock-Based Compensation (SBC):** SBC remains high at **$290.7 million** annually. While this is a non-cash expense that boosts operating cash flow, it causes share dilution.
*   **Share Repurchases:** Management is actively addressing dilution by repurchasing shares, spending **$329.1 million** in the latest fiscal year. This signals management's confidence in the current valuation.

#### **4. Balance Sheet Strength**
UiPath maintains a "fortress" balance sheet with significant liquidity and zero long-term debt.

*   **Liquidity:** The company holds **$871.2 million** in cash and **$601.3 million** in short-term investments, totaling **$1.47 billion** in total liquidity.
*   **Debt Profile:** The company is virtually debt-free, with no significant long-term borrowings beyond lease obligations.
*   **Current Ratio:** A very healthy current ratio (Current Assets of $2.24B vs. Current Liabilities of $0.9B) ensures the company can easily meet its short-term obligations and fund R&D.

#### **5. Valuation and Market Sentiment**
*   **Valuation Multiples:** With a **Forward P/E of 12.72** and a **PEG Ratio of 0.363**, PATH appears undervalued relative to its growth and peers in the technology sector.
*   **Market Position:** Trading at a Price-to-Sales (TTM) of **3.51**, which is conservative for a high-margin software firm that has achieved GAAP profitability.
*   **Analyst Outlook:** The consensus is a "Hold" (16 analysts), but the low PEG ratio and positive earnings shift suggest potential for upward revisions in analyst ratings and target prices (currently averaging $13.80).

---

### **Actionable Insights for Traders**
1.  **Profitability Catalyst:** The shift to GAAP profitability and positive operating income is a major de-risking event. Long-term investors may view the current valuation as an attractive entry point before the market fully prices in this fundamental shift.
2.  **Valuation Opportunity:** The PEG ratio of 0.363 is exceptionally low. If UiPath can maintain its 10-15% revenue growth while expanding margins, significant multiple expansion is possible.
3.  **Monitor SBC:** Traders should keep an eye on Stock-Based Compensation. While buybacks are currently offsetting dilution, a decrease in SBC or an increase in the buyback program would be a strong bullish signal.
4.  **M&A Potential:** With $1.47 billion in cash and no debt, UiPath is a prime candidate for strategic acquisitions to expand its AI and automation capabilities, or it could itself be an acquisition target for larger tech conglomerates.
