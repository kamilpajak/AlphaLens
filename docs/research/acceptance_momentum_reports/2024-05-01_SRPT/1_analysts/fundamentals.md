# Fundamental Analysis Report: Sarepta Therapeutics, Inc. (SRPT)
**Date:** May 1, 2024
**Ticker:** SRPT
**Sector:** Healthcare
**Industry:** Biotechnology

---

## 1. Company Profile
Sarepta Therapeutics, Inc. is a commercial-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on the discovery and development of unique RNA-targeted therapeutics, gene therapy, and other genetic medicine modalities for the treatment of rare diseases. The company is primarily known for its leadership in Duchenne muscular dystrophy (DMD), with several approved products (EXONDYS 51, VYONDYS 53, AMONDYS 45, and ELEVIDYS).

## 2. Basic Company Financials (as of Q1 2024)
*   **Market Capitalization:** ~$2.25 Billion
*   **Revenue (TTM):** ~$2.20 Billion
*   **Forward P/E Ratio:** 7.13
*   **Beta:** 0.283 (indicates low correlation with the broader market, typical for biotech)
*   **Cash and Short-term Investments:** ~$1.39 Billion (as of March 31, 2024)
*   **Debt-to-Equity Ratio:** 91.14

## 3. Financial Performance & History

### Revenue Growth
Sarepta has demonstrated explosive revenue growth over the last several years:
*   **2021:** $701.9 Million
*   **2022:** $933.0 Million
*   **2023:** $1.24 Billion
*   **Q1 2024:** $413.5 Million (putting them on track for >$1.6B annualized)

This growth is driven by the successful commercialization of its DMD portfolio and the recent launch of its gene therapy, ELEVIDYS.

### The Inflection Point: Profitability
The most critical fundamental development for SRPT is its recent transition to profitability:
*   **Q4 2023:** Reported a net income of **$45.66 Million**.
*   **Q1 2024:** Reported a net income of **$36.12 Million**.
For a biotechnology company that has historically operated at a significant loss due to heavy R&D investment, achieving two consecutive quarters of positive net income is a major de-risking event.

### Operating Expenses
*   **Research & Development (R&D):** Remains high at approximately $200M per quarter. However, the expanding revenue base now sufficiently covers these costs.
*   **Selling, General & Administrative (SG&A):** Stabilized around $120M-$130M per quarter, showing improved operating leverage as revenue scales.

## 4. Balance Sheet and Liquidity
Sarepta maintains a robust balance sheet to support its commercial operations and pipeline development:
*   **Liquidity:** With nearly **$1.4 Billion** in cash and short-term investments, the company has a strong buffer.
*   **Current Ratio:** 2.32, indicating very healthy short-term liquidity.
*   **Inventory:** Increased to **$373.5 Million** in Q1 2024 from $322.9 Million in Dec 2023, likely in anticipation of further ELEVIDYS demand.

## 5. Specific, Actionable Insights

### Positive Catalysts
1.  **Sustainable Profitability:** The transition from a "burning cash" biotech to a "self-sustaining" commercial entity significantly lowers the risk of dilutive equity raises in the near term.
2.  **Product Expansion:** Potential for label expansion for ELEVIDYS could drastically increase the addressable patient population, providing a multi-year growth runway.
3.  **Low Beta:** At 0.283, the stock may act as a defensive play within the healthcare sector during broader market volatility, as its valuation is driven more by clinical/regulatory outcomes than macroeconomic cycles.

### Risks to Monitor
1.  **Debt Levels:** While the company is now profitable, it still carries over $1.1B in long-term debt. Investors should monitor interest coverage ratios.
2.  **Regulatory Dependency:** As with all biotechs, significant revenue is tied to a small number of orphan drug approvals. Any regulatory setbacks or safety concerns would have a disproportionate impact on the stock.
3.  **Competition:** The DMD space is becoming increasingly competitive, which could impact long-term pricing power.
