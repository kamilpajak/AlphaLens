**Fundamental Analysis Report: FormFactor, Inc. (FORM)**

**Date of Analysis:** May 20, 2022
**Ticker:** FORM
**Sector/Industry:** Technology / Semiconductor Equipment & Materials

---

### **1. Company Profile & Overview**
FormFactor, Inc. is a leading provider of essential test and measurement technologies throughout the full semiconductor lifecycle—from characterization, modeling, reliability, and design debug to qualification and production test. The company's primary products include probe cards, analytical probes, probe stations, metrology systems, thermal systems, and cryogenic systems. Headquartered in Livermore, California, FormFactor serves major semiconductor companies and scientific institutions globally. Its products are critical for ensuring the quality and performance of semiconductor devices in markets like mobile, automotive, and high-performance computing.

### **2. Financial Performance (Income Statement Analysis)**
FormFactor has demonstrated robust revenue growth and a return to consistent profitability over the recent years:
*   **Revenue Growth:** Trailing Twelve Month (TTM) revenue as of mid-2022 stands at approximately **$785M**. Annual revenue grew from **$693.6M** in 2020 to **$769.7M** in 2021, representing an 11% year-over-year increase.
*   **Recent Quarterly Strength:** In Q1 2022 (ending March 31, 2022), the company reported revenue of **$197.2M**, a significant performance indicating a strong start to the year.
*   **Profitability:** Net income for fiscal year 2021 was **$83.9M**, up from **$78.5M** in 2020. The first quarter of 2022 showed a net income of **$29.9M**, suggesting an accelerating profit trend.
*   **Operating Efficiency:** The company maintains a healthy operating margin TTM of **13.6%** and a profit margin of **6.93%**.

### **3. Balance Sheet & Liquidity**
The company’s financial position is exceptionally stable, characterized by high liquidity and a conservative debt profile:
*   **Liquidity:** As of March 31, 2022, FormFactor held **$167.2M** in cash and equivalents, with total current assets of **$556.1M**.
*   **Current Ratio:** With current liabilities at **$154.7M**, the current ratio is approximately **3.59**, indicating an excellent ability to cover short-term obligations.
*   **Solvency:** Total shareholder equity stands at **$846.7M** against total liabilities of **$210.2M**. Long-term debt is minimal (around **$15.2M**), giving the company significant financial flexibility for acquisitions or internal R&D.

### **4. Cash Flow Analysis**
FormFactor is a strong cash generator, which is vital for a technology-intensive business:
*   **Operating Cash Flow (OCF):** In 2021, the company generated **$139.4M** in OCF, significantly higher than its net income, largely due to depreciation and stock-based compensation.
*   **Free Cash Flow (FCF):** After capital expenditures of **$66.5M** in 2021, the company generated an FCF of approximately **$72.9M**.
*   **Q1 2022 Performance:** The first quarter of 2022 saw **$44.2M** in OCF and **$28.6M** in FCF, maintaining the positive trend.

### **5. Market Valuation & Analyst Outlook**
*   **Valuation Ratios:** The current P/E ratio is high at **208.74**, though the Forward P/E is lower at **77.52**. This suggests the market has high growth expectations for the company. The Price to Sales (P/S) ratio TTM is **14.3**.
*   **Market Sentiment:** Analysts maintain a positive to neutral stance, with 4 "Buy" ratings and 6 "Hold" ratings. The average analyst target price is **$96**, which is significantly lower than current trading levels implied by the moving averages ($101.71 50-day MA), suggesting potential overvaluation or a recent rally that has outpaced analyst revisions.

### **6. Actionable Insights for Traders**
*   **Growth Play:** FORM is well-positioned to benefit from the ongoing global demand for semiconductor testing, especially as chip complexity increases.
*   **Valuation Risk:** The trailing P/E of 208 is aggressive. Traders should be cautious of "priced-for-perfection" scenarios where even slight earnings misses could trigger volatility.
*   **Solid Floor:** The fortress-like balance sheet and consistent positive free cash flow provide a safety net against long-term insolvency, making it a potentially attractive "buy-on-dips" candidate for those bullish on the semiconductor cycle.
*   **Technical Indicator:** The stock is currently trading above its 50-day and 200-day moving averages, signaling strong upward momentum. However, being near 52-week highs ($145.48) suggests looking for consolidation before entry.
