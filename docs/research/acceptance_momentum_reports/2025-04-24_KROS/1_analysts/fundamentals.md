# Fundamental Analysis Report: Keros Therapeutics, Inc. (KROS)

**Date of Analysis:** 2025-04-24
**Current Price (Est.):** ~$14.35 (Based on 50/200 Day Moving Averages and 52-week range)
**Ticker:** KROS

---

## 1. Company Profile
Keros Therapeutics, Inc. (KROS) is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company headquartered in Lexington, Massachusetts. The company focuses on the discovery, development, and commercialization of novel treatments for patients suffering from hematological and musculoskeletal disorders. Their pipeline is designed to address significant unmet medical needs by leveraging the body's natural signaling pathways to restore health in diseased tissues.

## 2. Financial Performance & Earnings Quality
KROS presents a unique financial profile for a clinical-stage biotech due to a massive revenue event in early 2025.

*   **Windfall Revenue (Q1 2025):** The company reported **$211.2 million** in revenue and **$148.5 million** in net income for the quarter ending March 31, 2025. This was likely a one-time milestone payment or licensing fee from a partnership agreement.
*   **Trailing 12-Month (TTM) Figures:**
    *   **Revenue:** $244.1 million.
    *   **Net Income:** $87.0 million.
    *   **EPS:** $2.30.
    *   **P/E Ratio:** 5.07.
*   **Operating Margins:** Despite the positive TTM net income, the **operating margin remains at -75.77%**. This discrepancy highlights that the company's core operations are still loss-making, and the positive earnings are entirely due to the Q1 windfall.
*   **Forward Outlook:** The **Forward P/E of 48.78** is a much more realistic valuation metric for traders, as analysts expect the company to return to net losses as R&D spending continues without recurring massive licensing fees.

## 3. Cash Flow & Liquidity Analysis
The company’s cash management over the past year has been extremely aggressive.

*   **Cash Position:** As of December 31, 2025, KROS held **$287.4 million** in cash and cash equivalents. This is a significant drop from the **$720.5 million** held in March 2025.
*   **The Buyback Factor:** The primary cause of this cash reduction was a massive **$375 million share repurchase** conducted in Q4 2025. For a clinical-stage biotech to spend nearly 50% of its peak cash on buybacks is a highly unusual and aggressive move.
*   **Operating Burn:** The company averages an operating cash burn of **$30 million to $45 million per quarter**, primarily driven by Research and Development ($129.6 million annually).
*   **Cash Runway:** At the current burn rate of ~$35M/quarter, the remaining $287.4M provides a **runway of approximately 8 quarters (2 years)**. This is sufficient to reach major clinical milestones but may require further capital raises if clinical trials are delayed.

## 4. Valuation & Market Sentiment
*   **Analyst Consensus:** 5 "Buy" ratings and 4 "Hold" ratings.
*   **Price Target:** The average analyst price target is **$22.57**, representing a potential upside of over 50% from current levels.
*   **Institutional Interest:** Ownership is reported at **103.57%**, suggesting high institutional conviction (and likely some double-counting due to short interest or reporting lags).
*   **Price Action:** The stock is currently trading below its 200-day moving average ($15.69) and 50-day moving average ($13.00), suggesting it may be finding a floor after the volatility of the buyback and windfall events.

## 5. Actionable Insights for Traders

1.  **Avoid the "Value Trap" PE:** Do not be misled by the P/E ratio of 5.07. This is a temporary artifact of the Q1 2025 milestone payment. The company is not yet sustainably profitable.
2.  **The Buyback Signal:** The $375M buyback is the most important fundamental signal. It suggests management believes the stock is significantly undervalued or that they have extremely high confidence in the upcoming clinical data for their lead candidates.
3.  **Monitor R&D Catalysts:** Since KROS is back to being a "burn-mode" biotech, the stock will move based on clinical trial data. Traders should identify the specific trial readouts expected in late 2025 and 2026.
4.  **Institutional Support:** With high institutional ownership, the stock is likely to be less volatile in "normal" periods but highly sensitive to any changes in the fundamental thesis that could cause a large fund to exit.
