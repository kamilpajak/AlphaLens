### **Fundamental Analysis Report: Krystal Biotech, Inc. (KRYS)**
**Date:** February 27, 2025
**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Biotechnology

---

#### **1. Executive Summary**
Krystal Biotech (KRYS) has successfully transitioned from a clinical-stage research firm to a highly profitable, commercial-stage powerhouse in the gene therapy sector. Following the 2023 approval and launch of **VYJUVEK** (the first redosable gene therapy for Dystrophic Epidermolysis Bullosa), the company has demonstrated explosive revenue growth and achieved consistent profitability. With a robust cash position, high institutional backing, and an expanding pipeline, KRYS represents a premium-growth biotech play with significantly lower financial risk than its peers.

---

#### **2. Financial Performance Analysis**

*   **Revenue Growth:** KRYS has achieved a rare trajectory in biotech. Revenue grew from $0 in 2022 to **$389.13 million** by the end of 2025. This 2024–2025 period alone saw a year-over-year revenue increase of approximately **17.5%**, underpinned by the successful commercial adoption of VYJUVEK.
*   **Profitability & Margins:**
    *   **Net Income:** The company turned profitable in 2023 ($10.9M, aided by a voucher sale) and surged to **$204.83 million** in 2025.
    *   **Operating Margin:** TTM operating margin stands at a healthy **41.3%**, showcasing an efficient commercial infrastructure.
    *   **Earnings Per Share (EPS):** TTM EPS of **$6.83** reflects the high-margin nature of gene therapies.
*   **Expense Management:** While revenue has soared, the company has maintained disciplined spending. R&D expenses were $58M in 2025, only a slight increase from $53.6M in 2024, indicating that the current infrastructure is scaling efficiently.

---

#### **3. Balance Sheet & Liquidity Analysis**

KRYS maintains an exceptionally clean and liquid balance sheet, which is a significant competitive advantage in a high-interest-rate environment.

*   **Cash Position:** Total cash and short-term investments reached **$496.3 million** as of Dec 31, 2025.
*   **Total Assets vs. Liabilities:** Total assets stand at **$1.33 billion**, while total liabilities are a mere **$114.2 million**.
*   **Debt Profile:** The company is virtually debt-free, with no long-term debt and only minor capital lease obligations ($9.3M). This allows KRYS to self-fund its pipeline and potential M&A activity without share dilution.
*   **Equity Growth:** Shareholder equity has grown steadily to **$1.22 billion**, reflecting retained earnings and a strong asset base.

---

#### **4. Cash Flow Analysis**

*   **Operating Cash Flow (OCF):** KRYS generated **$200.87 million** in OCF in 2025, up from $123.4 million in 2024. This confirms that its "earnings" are backed by actual cash inflows, a critical metric for long-term sustainability.
*   **Financing:** The company has moved away from needing capital markets for survival. Financing cash flow was minimal at $8.7M in 2025, contrasting with the $202.7M raised in 2023.

---

#### **5. Market Valuation & Analyst Outlook**

*   **Valuation Ratios:**
    *   **Trailing P/E:** 40.48
    *   **Forward P/E:** 47.39
    *   **Price-to-Sales (P/S):** 20.92
    *   **EV/EBITDA:** 43.21
    While these multiples are high, they are justified by the company's monopoly in its niche and its rapid transition to triple-digit net income growth.

*   **Analyst Target:** The consensus price target is **$314**, suggesting an upside potential of approximately **17-20%** from the current 50-day moving average levels.

---

#### **6. Actionable Insights for Traders**

*   **For Long-Term Investors:** KRYS is a "Core" biotech holding. The company has bypassed the "binary risk" phase and is now a cash-generating entity. Accumulation on dips toward the 200-day moving average ($212.24) offers a favorable entry.
*   **For Growth Traders:** The stock is exhibiting strong momentum, currently trading well above its 50-day ($262.82) and 200-day moving averages. Watch for breakouts toward the 52-week high of $298.3.
*   **Risk Mitigation:** The primary risk is pipeline stagnation or regulatory hurdles for its next set of candidates (e.g., cystic fibrosis). However, the cash-cow status of VYJUVEK provides a high floor for the stock price.
