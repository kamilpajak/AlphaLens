# Fundamental Analysis Report: Allegro MicroSystems, Inc. (ALGM)
**Date:** July 10, 2025
**Ticker:** ALGM
**Sector:** Technology | **Industry:** Semiconductors

## Executive Summary
Allegro MicroSystems (ALGM) is currently navigating a transitional phase following a significant downturn in fiscal year 2025 (ending March 31, 2025). While annual figures show a sharp decline in revenue and a swing to a net loss, recent quarterly data (ending June 30, 2025) suggests the company has reached a trough and is beginning a recovery. Traders should note the company's strong liquidity and continued aggressive investment in R&D, which positions it well for a cyclical rebound in the semiconductor sector, particularly in automotive and industrial sensing applications.

---

## 1. Company Profile & Strategic Positioning
Allegro MicroSystems is a leader in power and sensing solutions for the automotive and industrial markets. Their products—specifically magnetic sensors and power ICs—are critical components in electric vehicles (EVs), advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS), and green energy infrastructure.

**Strategic Insight:** Despite a rough 2024-2025, ALGM maintains a high Beta (1.695), indicating it will outperform in a bull market but carries higher risk during volatility. The company is doubling down on innovation, with Research & Development expenses remaining high at nearly $180M annually, even as revenues compressed.

## 2. Financial Performance Analysis

### Income Statement Trends
*   **Revenue Compression:** Annual revenue dropped ~31% from $1.05B (March 2024) to $725M (March 2025). This was primarily due to inventory corrections in the automotive and industrial sectors.
*   **Profitability:** The company moved from a net income of $152.7M in 2024 to a net loss of $73M in 2025.
*   **Quarterly Rebound:** Crucially, the most recent quarter (ending June 30, 2025) reported revenue of $203.4M, an improvement from the $166.9M seen in the same quarter the previous year. Although still showing a quarterly net loss of $13.2M, the upward revenue trajectory is a positive signal for traders.

### Balance Sheet Strength
*   **Liquidity:** ALGM maintains a robust **Current Ratio of 3.65**. With $121M in cash and equivalents and total current assets of $483M against only $112M in current liabilities, the company has no immediate solvency risks.
*   **Debt Profile:** The Debt-to-Equity ratio stands at a healthy 31.9%. Long-term debt increased to ~$345M in early 2025, likely used to sustain operations and R&D during the downturn.
*   **Inventory Management:** Inventory levels rose to $183.9M in March 2025 from $162.3M in 2024. Monitoring this for a decrease in subsequent quarters will be key to confirming the recovery.

### Cash Flow Dynamics
*   **Free Cash Flow (FCF):** TTM Free Cash Flow remains positive at **$113.2M**. This is a critical "safety net" metric, showing that despite accounting losses (partially due to non-cash items like depreciation of $64.5M and stock-based compensation of $41.9M), the core business still generates cash.

## 5. Trader’s Perspective & Actionable Outlook

*   **Bull Case:** If the automotive sector's transition to EVs accelerates and industrial inventory levels normalize, ALGM's high operating leverage could see earnings explode as revenue scales back toward the $1B mark. The positive quarterly revenue trend is the first "buy" signal.
*   **Bear Case:** If high interest rates continue to dampen EV demand, ALGM’s recovery could stall. Given the high Forward PE of 42, any miss in quarterly revenue growth could lead to a significant price correction.
*   **Recommendation:** Watch for the next quarterly earnings report. If revenue exceeds $215M and inventory begins to decline, it confirms the start of a new growth cycle.

FINAL TRANSACTION PROPOSAL: **HOLD** (Wait for confirmed quarterly revenue acceleration above $210M before increasing long positions).
