### Fundamental Analysis Report: C3.ai, Inc. (AI)
**Date of Analysis:** May 24, 2022
**Stock Ticker:** AI
**Exchange:** NYSE

---

#### 1. Company Profile
C3.ai, Inc. is a leading provider of Enterprise AI software. The company provides a platform-as-a-service (PaaS) and software-as-a-service (SaaS) applications that allow organizations to design, develop, and deploy large-scale AI, predictive analytics, and IoT applications. Their solutions target industries such as energy, manufacturing, financial services, and the public sector.

#### 2. Financial Performance (Fiscal Year Ending April 30, 2022)
Based on the most recent financial data (up to the April 2022 quarter), C3.ai is in a high-growth but heavy-loss phase.

*   **Revenue Growth:** Revenue has grown consistently from $91.6 million in FY2019 to $252.8 million in FY2022. This represents a robust Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR).
*   **Profitability Metrics:**
    *   **Gross Profit:** Gross profit for FY2022 was $189 million, representing a 74.8% gross margin. This indicates a strong core software business model.
    *   **Net Loss:** Net losses have widened significantly, reaching -$192 million in FY2022 compared to -$55.7 million in FY2021.
    *   **EBITDA:** -$453 million (TTM), reflecting high operational spending, particularly in Research and Development (R&D) and Sales and Marketing.
*   **EPS (TTM):** -$3.16.

#### 3. Balance Sheet & Liquidity
The company’s balance sheet remains its strongest fundamental pillar, largely due to its capital-raising activities (including its 2020 IPO).

*   **Cash Position:** As of April 30, 2022, C3.ai held approximately $339.5 million in cash and cash equivalents and an additional $620.6 million in short-term investments. Total liquidity is nearly **$960 million**.
*   **Debt:** The company has virtually no long-term debt, with only minor capital lease obligations ($3.6 million).
*   **Current Ratio:** With total current assets of $1.06 billion and current liabilities of $150 million, the current ratio is exceptionally high (~7.0x), indicating no immediate liquidity risks.

#### 4. Cash Flow Dynamics
*   **Operating Cash Flow:** FY2022 operating cash flow was -$86.5 million. While negative, it is significantly better than the GAAP net loss, primarily due to high non-cash expenses.
*   **Stock-Based Compensation (SBC):** SBC was a massive $113.4 million in FY2022. While this preserves cash, it causes substantial dilution for common shareholders and is a primary driver of the GAAP net losses.
*   **Capital Expenditures:** CapEx remains low ($4.3 million), typical for a software-based business.

#### 5. Market Valuation & Analyst Sentiment
*   **Market Cap:** ~$1.36 billion.
*   **P/S Ratio:** 4.43x (TTM). This is relatively low for the enterprise software sector, likely reflecting the market's concern over the pace of losses and SBC.
*   **Analyst Ratings:** The sentiment is cautious. There are 7 "Hold" ratings, 6 "Sell/Strong Sell" ratings, and only 1 "Buy" rating. The average target price is around $8.82, which is significantly below its current 200-day moving average ($15.38).

#### 6. Actionable Insights for Traders
*   **Risk of Dilution:** The high level of stock-based compensation (nearly 45% of total revenue) is a major headwind for long-term share price appreciation. Traders should monitor the "Shares Outstanding" trend, which grew from ~56 million in 2021 to ~104 million in 2022.
*   **Strong Cash Runway:** With nearly $1 billion in liquidity and an annual burn rate (OCF) of ~$86 million, the company has roughly **10 years of runway** at current levels. This mitigates bankruptcy risk but doesn't guarantee stock performance.
*   **Growth vs. Valuation:** A P/S of 4.4x suggests that much of the pessimism regarding losses may already be priced in. Any surprise shift toward "non-GAAP profitability" or "free cash flow positive" status could serve as a major upward catalyst.
*   **Technical Context:** The stock is trading well below its 200-day moving average ($15.38) and 50-day moving average ($9.24), indicating a strong downward trend. Traders should look for a consolidation base before attempting a long position.
