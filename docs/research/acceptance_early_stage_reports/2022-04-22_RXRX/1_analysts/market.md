Analysis of **RXRX** (Recursion Pharmaceuticals, Inc.) as of **April 22, 2022**, reveals a stock in a persistent and severe downtrend, currently testing critical historical support levels.

### Executive Summary
**RXRX** has experienced a significant decline since its 2021 highs, dropping from over $40 to its current levels near $6.14. Technical indicators across multiple timeframes suggest that the bearish momentum remains dominant. While the stock is approaching a potential support zone established in mid-March, there are no definitive signs of a trend reversal or a "bottoming" process.

---

### 1. Trend Analysis: Bearish Dominance
The trend indicators for **RXRX** present a grim outlook for the medium and long term:
*   **Medium-Term (50 SMA):** The 50-day Simple Moving Average stands at **$8.12**. With the current price at **$6.14**, the stock is trading approximately 24% below this average. The 50 SMA itself is sloping downward, acting as a ceiling for any relief rallies.
*   **Long-Term (200 SMA):** The 200-day SMA is significantly higher at **$17.96**. This massive gap indicates a deeply entrenched bear market. The stock has not traded above its 200 SMA since late 2021, suggesting institutional selling pressure.

### 2. Momentum Indicators: Negative Bias
Momentum remains firmly in the hands of the bears, though some indicators suggest a "stretched" condition:
*   **RSI (Relative Strength Index):** The RSI is currently **35.37**. While this is not yet in the "oversold" territory (below 30), it is trending downward from a mid-April peak of 51.69. This shows that the brief bullish attempt in early April has completely evaporated.
*   **MACD:** The MACD line (**-0.467**) is currently below the Signal line (**-0.460**), resulting in a negative histogram. This bearish crossover confirms that downward momentum is accelerating in the short term.

### 3. Volatility and Price Range
*   **Bollinger Bands:** The price of **$6.14** is gravitating toward the **Lower Bollinger Band ($5.91)**. Historically, a touch of the lower band can trigger a mean-reversion bounce, but in strong downtrends, price can "ride" the band lower.
*   **ATR (Average True Range):** The ATR has contracted to **0.626**, down from highs near 1.0 in March. This suggests that while the price is falling, the daily ranges are becoming smaller, often a sign of a "grinding" move lower rather than a capitulation event.

### 4. Support and Resistance Levels
*   **Critical Support:** The mid-March low of **$5.80** is the most significant floor. If **RXRX** fails to hold this level, it enters "price discovery" mode to the downside with no historical support beneath it.
*   **Immediate Resistance:** The first hurdle for bulls is the **$7.00 - $7.20** zone (recent consolidation), followed by the **50 SMA at $8.12**.

---

### Actionable Insights
*   **For New Buyers:** Avoid "catching a falling knife." There is no evidence of a trend change. A wait-and-see approach is recommended until the price can at least reclaim the 50-day SMA or form a higher low.
*   **For Current Holders:** The stock is testing its all-time lows. A break below **$5.80** on high volume would be a major sell signal, as it indicates the next leg of the bear market has begun.
*   **Risk Management:** Use the ATR to set stop-losses. A stop 2x ATR above the current price (~$1.25 above $6.14) would protect against short-covering spikes if holding a short position.
