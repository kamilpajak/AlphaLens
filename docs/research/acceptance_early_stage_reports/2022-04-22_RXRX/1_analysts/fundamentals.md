**Fundamental Analysis Report: Recursion Pharmaceuticals, Inc. (RXRX)**
**Date of Analysis:** April 22, 2022

### **1. Executive Summary**
Recursion Pharmaceuticals (RXRX) is a clinical-stage biotechnology company that distinguishes itself through its "Recursion OS," a vertically integrated platform combining biology, chemistry, automation, and data science to industrialize the drug discovery process. As of April 2022, the company remains in a high-growth, high-burn phase typical of platform-based biotech firms, supported by a robust cash position and strategic partnerships.

### **2. Company Profile & Strategic Positioning**
*   **Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Biotechnology
*   **Core Strategy:** Recursion aims to shift drug discovery from serendipitous observation to a systematic, data-driven "search" problem. Their platform generates one of the world's largest proprietary biological datasets, which is used to train machine learning models to identify new therapeutic candidates and predict drug-target interactions.
*   **Product Pipeline:** Focuses on rare diseases, oncology, and infectious diseases, with several candidates in early clinical trials.

### **3. Financial Performance Analysis (Trailing Data to Q1 2022)**

#### **Income Statement Highlights**
*   **Revenue Growth:** For the fiscal year ending Dec 31, 2021, RXRX reported $10.0M in revenue. The most recent quarter (Q1 2022, ending March 31) showed $5.3M in revenue, indicating a significant upward trajectory compared to the quarterly average of $2.5M in 2021. This revenue is primarily derived from collaboration agreements.
*   **Operating Loss:** Net losses for FY 2021 were $178.1M. Q1 2022 saw a net loss of $56.0M. The widening losses reflect aggressive scaling in Research & Development ($32.4M in Q1 2022) and SG&A ($21.1M in Q1 2022).
*   **Margins:** As a clinical-stage company, traditional profit margins are currently irrelevant; the focus is on R&D efficiency and pipeline progression.

#### **Balance Sheet & Liquidity**
*   **Cash Position:** As of March 31, 2022, RXRX held **$507.9M** in cash and cash equivalents, plus **$83.2M** in short-term investments, totaling **$591.1M** in immediate liquidity.
*   **Asset Strength:** Total assets stood at $734.3M, while total liabilities were $240.8M. The debt-to-equity ratio remains low, with long-term debt at approximately $0.6M.
*   **Cash Runway:** Based on a quarterly burn rate of roughly $55M-$60M, the company has a cash runway of approximately **10 quarters (2.5 years)**. This provides a substantial cushion to reach significant clinical milestones without immediate dilutive financing.

#### **Cash Flow Dynamics**
*   **Operating Cash Flow:** Net cash used in operating activities was -$158.6M in 2021. However, Q1 2022 showed a rare positive operating cash flow of $77.4M, likely due to a large upfront payment from a collaborator or changes in working capital (deferred revenue).
*   **Financing:** The company significantly bolstered its position via its IPO and subsequent financing in 2021, raising over $458M that year.

### **5. Actionable Insights for Traders**
*   **Long-Term Bull Case (Buy/Hold):** The "Cash Runway" is the strongest fundamental support. With 2.5 years of funding, RXRX can survive a volatile biotech market. Investors should focus on clinical trial readouts for REC-994 and REC-2282 as primary catalysts.
*   **Risk Factors (Caution):** The high burn rate and reliance on a platform that has yet to produce a commercialized drug mean the company is a high-risk "story" stock. Any failure in the lead clinical programs could lead to a sharp re-valuation of the platform's worth.
*   **Trading Strategy:** The stock appears undervalued relative to analyst targets ($6.71 vs ~$3.50). Traders might look for a "double bottom" near the 52-week low ($2.80) to enter positions, or wait for a breakout above the 50-day moving average ($3.45) on high volume.
