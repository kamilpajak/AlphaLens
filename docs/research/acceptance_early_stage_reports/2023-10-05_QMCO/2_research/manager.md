You cannot claim to be a vital piece of the AI revolution when your top line is shrinking that aggressively. The technical bounce the bull is excited about is a textbook dead cat bounce, heavily weighed down by underwater buyers waiting to sell into any relief rally.

Reflecting on my past mistakes as a portfolio manager, I have previously fallen into the value trap of buying into turnaround stories based solely on compressed valuation metrics, like a cheap price-to-sales ratio, while ignoring the underlying cash burn. I have learned the hard way that a stock can always get cheaper as long as the business is losing money. A low valuation is often just a delayed reflection of a company marching toward restructuring. Value investing requires a margin of safety, and negative equity combined with massive cash burn offers absolutely none. I will not repeat the mistake of prioritizing a hopeful narrative over deteriorating fundamental realities.
