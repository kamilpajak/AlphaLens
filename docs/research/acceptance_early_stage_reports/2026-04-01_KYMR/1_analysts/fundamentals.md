### Fundamental Analysis Report: Kymera Therapeutics (KYMR)
**Date:** April 1, 2026
**Ticker:** KYMR (NASDAQ)
**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Biotechnology

---

#### 1. Executive Summary
Kymera Therapeutics (KYMR) is a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company pioneering targeted protein degradation (TPD) to treat diseases previously considered "undruggable." As of April 2026, Kymera presents a robust fundamental profile characterized by a **massive cash reserve** following a significant 2025 financing round, aggressive R&D investment, and overwhelming institutional support. While the company remains pre-profitability—typical for biotech—its financial runway is exceptionally long, providing a multi-year safety net for clinical execution.

#### 2. Financial Health & Liquidity
Kymera’s most striking fundamental feature is its liquidity position.
*   **Total Liquidity:** As of December 31, 2025, the company held **$1.62 billion** in cash, short-term, and long-term investments (Cash & ST: ~$848M, LT: ~$771M).
*   **Cash Runway:** In 2025, Kymera reported an operating cash burn of approximately **$232.9 million**. At this burn rate, the company has a **runway of 6–7 years**, placing it in the top tier of well-capitalized biotech firms.
*   **Recent Financing:** The company successfully raised **$990.7 million** through financing in 2025, significantly diluting shares (from ~58M in 2023 to ~84M in 2025) but effectively removing near-term insolvency or "emergency" capital raise risks.
*   **Current Ratio:** A stellar **10.4x**, indicating immediate obligations are easily covered.

#### 3. Operational Performance & R&D
*   **Revenue Volatility:** TTM Revenue stands at **$39.2 million**. Quarterly revenue is highly variable (ranging from $2.8M to $22.1M in 2025), which is indicative of milestone-based income from pharmaceutical partnerships (e.g., Sanofi).
*   **R&D Commitment:** R&D expenses rose to **$316.6 million** in 2025 (up from $240M in 2024). This 32% YoY increase suggests a rapid expansion of clinical trials or the advancement of their lead protein degrader candidates into late-stage development.
*   **Net Losses:** The net loss for 2025 was **$311.4 million** ($3.69 per share). Investors should view this not as a failure of the business model, but as the "cost of manufacturing" intellectual property in the biotech sector.

#### 4. Valuation & Market Sentiment
*   **Price Action:** KYMR has shown high volatility (Beta: 2.29), trading between $28 and $103 over the last year. It is currently consolidating near its 50-day moving average ($84.03).
*   **Valuation Ratios:**
    *   **Price-to-Book (P/B):** 4.53x. For a biotech with $1.6B in cash, this is a relatively grounded valuation.
    *   **Market Cap:** ~$7.1 billion.
*   **Analyst Consensus:** Extremely bullish. Of 23 analysts, 22 have "Buy" or "Strong Buy" ratings. The average target price of **$119.14** implies an upside potential of approximately **40%**.
*   **Institutional Ownership:** Reported at **110.5%** (reflecting high conviction, though figures over 100% often involve short interest or reporting lags). This suggests the stock is a "staple" for biotech-focused institutional portfolios.

#### 5. Strategic Risks
*   **Clinical Success:** As with all biotechs, the primary risk is clinical failure. Large R&D spends do not guarantee FDA approval.
*   **Dilution:** While the 2025 raise provided a safety net, it diluted existing shareholders by roughly 40% compared to 2023 levels.
*   **High Beta:** The stock will likely swing more violently than the broader market during periods of macro-economic uncertainty.

#### 6. Actionable Insights for Traders
*   **For Long-term Investors:** The 6-year cash runway makes KYMR a lower-risk "platform play" in the protein degradation space. The massive cash position allows management to weather market downturns without needing to raise capital at unfavorable prices.
*   **For Swing Traders:** The stock is currently trading below its 50-day and above its 200-day moving averages. A breakout above the $85–$90 range could signal a return toward the $103 high, supported by the strong analyst target price of $119.
*   **Monitoring Catalyst:** Watch for quarterly revenue spikes, which often signal milestone achievements in their partnership programs, and clinical trial data readouts which are the primary drivers of the $7B+ market cap.
