Hello everyone. After listening to both sides of the debate on CEVA, I have evaluated the arguments and am ready to make a definitive decision.

The bull and bear presented vastly different pictures of the company. The bull analyst argued that CEVA is a coiled spring, highlighting their massive cash position of 222 million dollars, which represents about a third of their market cap. They pointed out the impressive 87 percent gross margins and argued that the heavy R&D spending is a necessary, calculated investment for capturing the Edge AI and Wi-Fi 7 markets. On the technical side, the bull emphasized the recent momentum, noting that the stock has reclaimed its 50 day moving average and is showing signs of a breakout.

On the flip side, the bear analyst brought a crucial dose of reality. They highlighted that despite the high gross margins, CEVA is operating at a net loss, pouring 68 percent of its revenue into R&D while top line revenue remains entirely stagnant. The bear also made a highly compelling point about the macroeconomic environment. While CEVA licenses intellectual property and does not manufacture chips directly, their customers are chipmakers. If those customers get squeezed by tariffs and rising manufacturing costs, they will inevitably delay new design starts, which directly chokes the licensing revenue pipeline for CEVA. Finally, the bear pointed out that the stock is still trading well below its 200 day moving average, making this recent bounce look more like a classic bull trap than a sustainable reversal.

My recommendation for CEVA is a decisive Sell.

The rationale comes down to the fundamental disconnect between the AI narrative and the actual financial performance of the company. In the middle of an unprecedented semiconductor boom, a company positioned exactly where the industry is heading should not be struggling with flat revenue. The bull wants us to believe the heavy R&D spend will eventually pay off, but without accelerating top line growth, it operates as a money pit. Additionally, the downstream effect of tariffs on the customer base of CEVA is a realistic and heavy headwind. A top of the funnel licensing business cannot thrive if the downstream manufacturers are stalling their projects to protect their own margins.

Reflecting on my own past mistakes as a portfolio manager, I have previously fallen into the trap of buying small cap tech stocks simply because they had a fortress balance sheet and a compelling future narrative. I used to look at a massive cash pile and assume it provided an unbreakable floor for the stock price. What I learned the hard way is that the market does not assign full value to cash if the company is consistently burning through it without delivering measurable top line growth. A large cash balance in a stagnant business is just a longer runway for mediocrity, not a catalyst for shareholder returns. I will not repeat that mistake by getting lured in by the balance sheet of CEVA while ignoring its deteriorating income statement.
