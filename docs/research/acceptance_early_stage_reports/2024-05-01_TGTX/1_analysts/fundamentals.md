# Fundamental Analysis Report: TG Therapeutics, Inc. (TGTX)
**Date:** May 1, 2024
**Ticker:** TGTX (NASDAQ)
**Sector:** Healthcare | **Industry:** Biotechnology

---

## 1. Company Profile
TG Therapeutics, Inc. (TGTX) is a commercial-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on developing and commercializing novel treatments for B-cell malignancies and autoimmune diseases. The company's primary commercial asset is **BRIUMVI® (ublituximab-xiiy)**, which received FDA approval in late 2022 for the treatment of relapsing forms of multiple sclerosis (RMS) in adults. TGTX is currently transitioning from a research-intensive firm to a high-growth commercial entity, leveraging its position in the highly competitive but lucrative MS market.

## 2. Financial Performance (Income Statement Analysis)
TGTX has seen a meteoric rise in revenue following the commercial launch of Briumvi.

*   **Explosive Revenue Growth:**
    *   **Q1 2024 (Ending March 31):** Reported **$63.47 million** in total revenue, compared to just $7.8 million in Q1 2023. This represents an ~8x year-over-year increase, signaling strong market uptake.
    *   **2023 Full Year:** The company achieved **$233.66 million** in revenue, a massive jump from $2.78 million in 2022. Note: Q3 2023 revenue of $165.8 million was significantly bolstered by a one-time upfront payment from a licensing agreement with Neuraxpharm for rights to Briumvi outside the U.S.
*   **Path to Profitability:**
    *   **Operating Loss narrowing:** Q1 2024 operating income was **-$9.27 million**, a significant improvement from the heavy losses in 2021/2022 when R&D was peak and revenue was non-existent.
    *   **Margins:** TTM Gross Profit Margin is high (~83% as of Q1 2024), typical for successfully scaled specialty biotechs. Profit margins have been skewed recently by licensing income (72.6% TTM), but normalized margins are trending toward break-even.

## 3. Balance Sheet & Cash Flow
*   **Liquidity & Capital:** As of March 31, 2024, TGTX reported **$209.78 million** in cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments. This provides a substantial runway to fund commercialization efforts without immediate dilution.
*   **Debt Management:** Total Long-Term Debt stands at **$101.3 million**. The company has managed to maintain a relatively clean capital structure compared to many peers that rely heavily on convertible debt.
*   **Cash Flow Trends:**
    *   **Operating Cash Flow:** Q1 2024 cash burn from operations was only **$8.2 million**, down significantly from the $81.3 million burn in Q1 2021.
    *   **Financing:** The company has been successful in raising capital through equity and strategic partnerships rather than toxic debt, evidenced by the Neuraxpharm deal which provided non-dilutive capital.

## 4. Market Position & Valuation
*   **Market Cap:** ~$5.9 Billion.
*   **Valuation Multiples:**
    *   **Trailing P/E:** 13.34 (Note: This is "artificially" low due to the licensing gain).
    *   **Forward P/E:** 30.58 (Reflects expected recurring earnings from Briumvi).
    *   **Price to Sales (TTM):** 9.58x. For a biotech company growing revenue at this pace, a P/S under 10 is often considered attractive.
*   **Volatility:** Beta of **1.755** indicates significantly higher volatility than the broader market, which is standard for commercial-stage biotech.

## 6. Actionable Insights for Traders

1.  **Sustained Commercial Momentum:** The primary catalyst for TGTX is the continued prescription growth of Briumvi. Q1 2024's $63M revenue confirms the product is not a "one-quarter wonder." Traders should monitor quarterly script data; any acceleration beyond $70M in Q2 would likely spark a breakout.
2.  **Near-Term Profitability Catalyst:** TGTX is on the verge of becoming "GAAP profitable" on a recurring basis. Achieving consistent positive EPS without licensing help will likely lead to a re-rating of the stock toward the $40+ level.
3.  **High Short Interest/Volatility Play:** Given the high Beta and the binary nature of biotech growth, the stock is sensitive to macro healthcare sentiment. However, the strong cash position (~$210M) mitigates the "death spiral" risk common in smaller biotechs.
4.  **Strategic M&A Candidate:** As a single-product success story with a strong foothold in the MS market, TGTX remains a potential acquisition target for larger pharmaceutical companies looking to bolster their neurology portfolios.
